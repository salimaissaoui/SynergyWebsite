Introduction to PFAS

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are synthetic chemicals known for their resistance to water, grease, and heat. Common examples include:

  • PFOS: Used in firefighting foams and water-resistant coatings.
  • PFOA: Found in non-stick cookware like Teflon.

There are over 12,000 variants of PFAS.
