Brief Introduction to PFAS

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are a class of synthetic chemicals widely used in industrial applications due to their resistance to water, grease, and heat. Among the most well-known PFAS compounds are Perfluorooctane Sulfonate (PFOS) and Perfluorooctanoic Acid (PFOA).

PFOS: Used in firefighting foams and water-resistant coatings

PFOA: Key component in non-stick cookware like Teflon

There are more than 12,000 different variants of PFAS.

Industries Impacted by PFAS

Industrial Manufacturing Plants

Soil and water leaching, air pollution from manufacturing processes.

Electronic Manufacturing Plants

Soil and water leaching from electronic component production.

Landfills

Soil and water leaching from disposed PFAS-containing products.

Airports

Soil and water contamination from firefighting foams.

Sources of Exposure and Long-Term Effects

PFAS chemicals are widely utilized across multiple industries and have been shown to leach into our daily lives in many different ways, directly impacting not just our communities, but our loved ones.

Negative Health Effects:

  • Cancer
  • Hormonal Disruption
  • Immune System Impacts
  • Thyroid Disease
