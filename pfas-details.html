About PFAS

What is PFAS?

PFAS, or Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances, are a large class of synthetic chemicals known for their durability and resistance to water, oil, and heat. Unfortunately, PFAS are also known as "forever chemicals" because they persist in the environment and accumulate in living organisms over time.

  • PFOS: Used in firefighting foams and stain-resistant coatings.
  • PFOA: Used in the production of non-stick cookware, such as Teflon.

Industries That Pollute

PFAS pollution can be traced to several industries due to their extensive use in manufacturing and production processes, including:

  • Textile and Apparel
  • Non-Stick Cookware
  • Firefighting
  • Food Packaging
  • Electronics Manufacturing

Health Effects of PFAS

Prolonged exposure to PFAS has been associated with various health issues, such as:

  • Increased Cholesterol Levels
  • Immune System Suppression
  • Thyroid Disorders
  • Certain Cancers
  • Developmental Delays
